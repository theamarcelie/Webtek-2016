History

Though fish leather may seem like an innovative and exotic material for the future, the practice of making fish leather is ancient. Hunters and gatherers in Iceland have traditions from centuries ago of making shoes out of fish skin. Another example are tribes from east-Asia, such as the Nanai people, who's been making traditional clothes made of fish leather since the 18th century.


Countries that already have established salmon industries, such as Ireland, Chile and Alaska, also have a tradition of producing and manufactering fish leather. Some common uses are clothing, gloves and shoes, cover for books and cell phones, and even as to upholster seats in cars or boats.
