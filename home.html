Why Salmon skin?

Here in Norway we’ve had a close connection with the ocean since our ancestors settled along the far-stretching coastline that twists and twirls itself upwards from Halden to Kirkenes. For a long time, the generations that grew up along these shores were never used to great financial wealth, and lived from hand to mouth. Most of them probably never saw any diamonds or gemstones, but they had something equally beautiful: the brilliance and glimmer of the catch as the fisherman hoists it up from the depths, revealing the nourishing contents that would provide for the family back home. Over the years we’ve learned to appreciate the bounties that the ocean provides for us, but now it’s time to start giving back. Climate change is a grave threat for the ocean and the life it shelters, and we as a society needs to adjust our consumption and start using more environmentally friendly materials if we wish to continue reaping the benefits of the sea. This is why I wish to produce salmon skin as a material, to give consumers a sustainable alternative by doing what we Norwegians do best: utilizing the resources that the ocean provides for us.

-Peder Holm, founder of Lakseskinn
